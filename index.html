About Me

I'm a senior research fellow at MIT Blueprint Labs working with Parag Pathak, focusing on the design and implementation of equitable assignment mechanisms in education. My research interests lie at the intersection of education, labor, and social impact, where I use tools from Economics, Machine Learning, and Algorithm Design to address systemic challenges and develop solutions that bridge theoretical insights with practical applications.

Previously, I interned as a Data Engineer at Tresata, where I worked on large-scale data analysis problems. I also conducted research with Dr. Mark Foley on the economic impacts of delayed graduation, a project inspired by COVID-19's effects on educational outcomes. I graduated from Davidson College with a background in Economics, Computer Science, and Mathematics.

Reading

I enjoy reading books at the intersection of economics, education, and social change. Some of my favorite works include "Who Gets What — and Why" by Alvin Roth, which explores the fascinating world of market design and matching mechanisms. I'm also deeply influenced by "Ghosts in the Schoolyard" by Eve L. Ewing, which provides crucial insights into school closures and their community impact.
